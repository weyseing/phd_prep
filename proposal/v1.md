## Section 3 — One-page research summary (attached PDF)

The one-page research summary is generated as a PDF and attached to every outreach email.

**Files:**
- 📎 PDF (attach this to email): [research_summary.pdf](research_summary.pdf)
- ✏️ Editable source (markdown): [research_summary.md](research_summary.md)
- 🎨 Styled HTML (regenerate PDF from this): [research_summary.html](research_summary.html)

**To regenerate the PDF after editing:**

1. Edit `research_summary.md` (content) or `research_summary.html` (layout).
2. Run from the `proposal/` folder:
   ```bash
   "/Applications/Google Chrome.app/Contents/MacOS/Google Chrome" \
     --headless --disable-gpu --no-pdf-header-footer \
     --print-to-pdf=research_summary.pdf \
     "file://$PWD/research_summary.html"
   ```
3. The new `research_summary.pdf` is the file to attach to outreach emails.

## Section 4 — Pre-send checklist (every email, every time)

**1. Research the professor:**
- [ ] Read 1–2 of their recent papers (last 2 years preferred)
- [ ] Verify correct salutation/title on their faculty page (some prefer "Dr." or "Associate Professor")

**2. Tailor the email:**
- [ ] Pick the right template (applied vs research) — when in doubt, applied is safer
- [ ] Replace `[specific paper / lab focus]` with the actual paper title or research theme
- [ ] Replace `[specific connection]` with a specific link to your direction (generic flattery is detectable and ignored)

**3. Attachments and signature:**
- [ ] CV (PDF) up-to-date and attached
- [ ] `research_summary.pdf` attached
- [ ] Signature includes name, email, LinkedIn, GitHub

**4. Final checks before hitting send:**
- [ ] Re-read the email aloud once (catches awkward phrasing)
- [ ] Logged in `outreach/sent_log.md` (date, prof, university, template version, response status)
