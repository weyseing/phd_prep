# Field 1: JEPA / World Models
**Paper:** A Path Towards Autonomous Machine Intelligence (2022 — Yann LeCun) — https://openreview.net/forum?id=BZ5a1r-kVsf

## TL;DR
LeCun's position paper for how to build agents that learn the world like animals do: a modular cognitive architecture whose engine is a Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture (JEPA) — twin encoders plus a predictor that learns latent dynamics non-contrastively, stacked hierarchically (H-JEPA) for multi-time-scale prediction, and used inside a Mode-2 model-predictive planner. The paper's signature claim is that prediction should happen in abstract representation space, not in raw input space.

## What it does (the mechanism)
The cognitive architecture has six modules wired together with differentiable signals: a **perception** module that encodes sensors into a state s, a **world model** that predicts future states (and fills in unobserved current state), an **actor** that proposes action sequences, a **cost** module split into a hard-wired **intrinsic cost** (pain, hunger, curiosity drives) and a trainable **critic** that predicts future intrinsic cost, a **short-term memory** (a key-value associative store, hippocampus-like) used to train the critic, and a **configurator** that re-parameterises every other module on the fly for the current task. Two operating modes: **Mode-1** is reactive (perception → policy → action, no rollout); **Mode-2** is deliberate model-predictive control — the actor proposes (a[0]…a[T]), the world model rolls out (s[1]…s[T]), the cost integrates energy along the trajectory, gradients of total cost flow back through the rollout to the action variables, the actor optimises, executes the first action, repeats.

The world-model engine itself is a **JEPA**. Two encoders gx, gy produce sx = gx(x) (past/context) and sy = gy(y) (future/target). A **predictor** outputs ŝy = Pred(sx, z) where z is a **latent variable** carrying everything about y that cannot be inferred from x — e.g. whether the car at the fork went left or right. The **energy** is the prediction error in latent space: F(x, y) = D(sy, Pred(sx, z̆)) with z̆ = argmin_z over an admissible set. **H-JEPA** stacks JEPAs: JEPA-1 encodes fine-grained state and predicts short-horizon; JEPA-2 takes JEPA-1's representations as input and predicts longer-horizon over a coarser, more invariant code. Higher levels drop unpredictable detail by design — abstraction emerges as a consequence of long-horizon predictability.

## Why it matters
This is a clean break from two dominant paradigms. **LLMs** predict tokens in input space — fine for discrete text, ruinous for high-bandwidth sensory streams where most pixels are noise (carpet texture, leaves in wind). **Generative models** (diffusion, autoencoders, VAEs) commit to reconstructing y in full, paying a curse-of-dimensionality tax for detail nobody needs. JEPA sidesteps both: predict in latent space, let the encoder discard whatever isn't predictable, and use latent z as a budget-limited slot for genuine epistemic uncertainty. The paper also argues forcefully that **non-contrastive** SSL is the right training regime — contrastive losses need exponentially many negatives in high dimension, while regularizers on representation variance/covariance (VICReg, Barlow Twins) avoid that. This blueprint seeded the entire JEPA program: I-JEPA (images), V-JEPA (video), MC-JEPA (motion+content), and follow-ons in robotics. Today most "world model" discourse outside RL traces back here.

## Key ideas to remember
- **Mode-1 vs Mode-2** maps to Kahneman System 1/2: reactive policy vs deliberate rollout-and-optimise (model-predictive control).
- **Informational collapse** is the central failure mode of joint-embedding training: encoders shortcut to constant outputs, energy goes flat. Prevent it by maximising info content of sx and sy (variance + decorrelation), minimising info content of z (sparsity/discretisation/noise), and forcing predictability sy ≈ Pred(sx, z).
- **Energy-based, not probabilistic.** F(x, y) is a scalar compatibility score. No Gibbs normalisation required. EBMs naturally represent multi-modal y|x.
- **Non-contrastive SSL** (VICReg, Barlow Twins) is "contrastive over feature dimensions" rather than over samples — the regularizer shrink-wraps low-energy regions onto the data manifold without negative mining.
- **Reasoning ≡ energy minimisation** over a differentiable graph (factor-graph view).

## Vocabulary glossary
- **JEPA** — Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture: twin encoders + predictor; energy is latent-space prediction error.
- **H-JEPA** — hierarchical stack of JEPAs operating at successively coarser time scales / more abstract representations.
- **encoder sx / sy** — the two embedding branches; not required to share weights or even modality.
- **predictor** — small network mapping sx (and z) to ŝy.
- **latent z** — uncertainty slot; carries information about y that x cannot supply; must be regularised to prevent collapse.
- **EBM (energy-based model)** — F(x, y) scalar, low on data, high elsewhere; no probability normalisation needed.
- **informational collapse** — degenerate solution where encoders ignore inputs; energy becomes flat.
- **VICReg** — Variance-Invariance-Covariance Regularization; non-contrastive loss enforcing component-wise variance + decorrelation on embeddings.
- **configurator** — executive-control module that re-parameterises perception, world model, actor, cost for the current task.
- **Mode-2 reasoning** — deliberate planning by rolling out the world model and minimising integrated cost over actions (model-predictive control with learned model).
- **intrinsic cost** — hard-wired, immutable scalar driver of behaviour (pain/hunger/curiosity); the only place priors live.

## Limitations / honest take
This is a position paper, not an empirical one. No benchmarks, no codebase, no ablations — every figure is a block diagram. Several mechanisms are speculative: the configurator is hand-waved (how does it actually re-parameterise other nets at inference time?), the intrinsic-cost design problem is essentially unsolved, and the leap from a single-step JEPA to a working H-JEPA planner is much larger than the paper concedes. JEPA itself was only operationalised after publication — I-JEPA (2023), V-JEPA (2024), MC-JEPA — and even those don't yet do Mode-2 planning end-to-end. Read this as a manifesto and a research agenda, not a result.

## Fit to my proposal
**Verdict:** main angle.

The proposal — encoder for online community state plus latent-dynamics predictor — *is* a JEPA, full stop. Cite this as the architectural blueprint and the conceptual anchor of the thesis. Several specific moves follow. **(a) H-JEPA = the hierarchy of community scales.** JEPA-1 encodes a thread (post-level dynamics, hours); JEPA-2 encodes a subreddit's daily state from JEPA-1's outputs; JEPA-3 spans the cross-subreddit ecosystem on weekly/monthly horizons. This maps cleanly onto the multi-scale structure of online discourse and gives a principled reason for the hierarchy beyond "deep is good." **(b) Latent z = unobserved drivers.** Real-world mental-health discourse is shaped by triggers we don't see in the corpus: news events, viral content elsewhere on the internet, undiagnosed shifts in user populations, platform-policy changes. JEPA's z slot is built exactly for this — and it is the natural locus for counterfactuals. "What if z had been different?" becomes a tractable computation: hold sx fixed, sweep z, decode (or just compare ŝy trajectories). **(c) Mode-2 = intervention simulation.** Treat moderation strategies, public-health messaging, content-policy changes as actions. Use the learned world model to roll out alternative trajectories under candidate interventions and rank by an intrinsic cost (e.g. forecasted crisis-signal density). This is the third leg of the proposal — and it gets a clean mathematical formulation here. **(d) Non-generative is the right call for text.** Autoregressively generating counterfactual posts is a dead end (LLM hallucination, evaluation impossible). Predicting in latent space is the principled escape. **(e) Training loss.** Cite VICReg / Barlow Twins as the SSL objective for community-state encoders.
